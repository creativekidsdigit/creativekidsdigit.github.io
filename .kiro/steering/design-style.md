# Fearless Creations — Visual Style Direction

## Style in one line
**Clean + modern + friendly, with a slightly playful touch.** Warm and approachable for parents, simple layouts, soft colors, professional but not too formal.

## Audience
- Parents (esp. of neurodivergent kids), teachers, busy mothers
- Should feel calm, trustworthy, and welcoming — never corporate or cold

## Visual rules of thumb

**Layout**
- Generous whitespace, simple grids, content max-width ~1180px
- Soft rounded corners (16–32px), gentle shadows — never sharp or heavy
- Sections breathe — alternate subtle pastel washes for rhythm

**Color**
- Lavender-led soft purple base (`--lavender #E8DEF7`, `--lilac #C8B6E2`, `--plum #6E5AA0`)
- Pastel accents only: blush, peach, mint, butter, sky
- Avoid: saturated, neon, primary-bright colors

**Typography**
- Headings: Fraunces (warm, slightly editorial serif) — italic for friendly emphasis
- Body: Plus Jakarta Sans (rounded, modern sans)
- Sentence case in nav and CTAs, not ALL CAPS (except small eyebrows)

**Voice & copy**
- Warm, calm, plainspoken. "One soft email a week" > "Subscribe now"
- Never urgent/sales-y. Avoid exclamation marks and pushy CTAs.

**Playful touches (use sparingly)**
- Hand-drawn-feel wavy underlines for emphasized words in headlines
- Small sparkle accents near the logo or special CTAs
- Gentle hover micro-interactions (rotate, lift, soft shadow)
- Animated floating product cards in hero (with `prefers-reduced-motion` honored)

**Imagery**
- Hand-drawn or softly illustrated > photographic
- If photos are used: soft natural light, pastel palette, no stock-y vibes

## Anti-patterns (avoid)
- Hard black text on hard white — use `--ink #2E2640` on cream
- Sharp 90° corners, harsh dividers
- Gradients with high contrast or vivid hues
- Overly clever/luxe minimalism that feels cold
- Cartoony or childish illustration that undermines trust
