What the Research Actually Says Works

According to the CDC and peer-reviewed educational research, the most effective classroom interventions for ADHD combine three pillars simultaneously:

  1. Environmental modifications — Seating, visual schedules, reduced cognitive load
  2. Instructional adaptations — Task chunking, explicit modeling, worked examples
  3. Positive behavioral support — Immediate feedback, Daily Report Cards, reward systems
