## 1. The transformation promise (locked)

**Before this kit:** Mornings end in yelling. Bedtime takes 90 minutes. Homework is a nightly battle. The 4pm meltdown blindsides the family. Lost folders. Forgotten papers. Anxiety hidden as anger. A parent who feels like they're failing.

**After this kit:** A printed routine on the bedroom door at 7am. A 5-step bedtime that actually lands. Homework in 5 short blocks with movement between each. A focus tracker that finds your kid's best hour. A reward chart that celebrates effort, not perfection. A ready-to-send teacher letter and a log that saves your case if you ever escalate. A parent who has the right page in their hand at the moment they need it.

**The single sentence:**

> *The ADHD Back-to-School Success Kit gives ADHD families one calm, complete printable system for the entire school year — built for the 9pm tired parent, designed for the ADHD brain, and ready to print tonight.*

## 3. ADHD-friendly design system (locked)

| Element | Spec |
|---|---|
| Visual rule | One concept per page. Single column. Max 6 chunks per page. |
| Palette | Primary sage `#A8C5A8`, cream `#FAF6EE`, terracotta `#D49A7C`, butter `#E8D88A`, charcoal text `#3A3A3A` |
| Typography | Headings: Fraunces 600/700. Body: Inter 400/600. Kid-facing accent: Caveat 500. Max 2 type sizes per page. |
| Whitespace | 0.75-in margins. 1.6+ line height. No page more than 60% inked. |
| Reusability | Designed to laminate and reuse with dry-erase. Soft gray `#E0DCD2` rules on every fillable line. |
| Reading level | Adult: 7th grade. Kid-facing: 3rd grade, present tense, "I" statements. |
| No-no list | No clipart cartoons, no stock photos, no rainbow gradients, no clutter, no decorative borders. |

### Page 2 — How to Use This Kit

**Header:** Start Here

**Body:**

This is 11 printables in one PDF. You don't need to print them all today. Print the page that matches the hardest part of your week, and start there.

**Most families print in this order:**

1. **Tonight (10 min):** the Morning Routine + Bedtime Routine.
2. **This weekend:** the Weekly Planner + Backpack Checklist + School Supply Checklist.
3. **First school day:** the Homework Planner + Focus Tracker.
4. **First week:** the Reward Chart + Emotion Regulation worksheet.
5. **First month:** the Parent–Teacher Log + Goal-Setting Worksheet.

**One rule:** if a page isn't working after 7 days, stop using it. Try a different page. The kit is a menu, not a homework assignment.

**You are allowed to:** lower the bar. Print one page. Skip what doesn't fit. Come back when you're ready.

**LAYOUT:** Two-column. Left: numbered timeline with icons. Right: butter-yellow callout with the "one rule" + permission line.

### Page 4 — Bedtime Routine Chart

**Header (kid-facing):** My Bedtime
**Subhead:** Wind down in 5 steps. Lights low. No new demands.

**Body — 5 vertical steps:**

1. **Snack & water** — small protein, one glass
2. **Bath or shower** — warm, dim light, no screens
3. **PJs + brush teeth** — set out tomorrow's clothes
4. **Quiet input** — book, audiobook, drawing, lego
5. **Lights low + connection** — story, song, or 5 min together

**Wind-down "yes" list:**
- Audiobook
- Weighted blanket
- Stuffed animal
- White noise / fan
- Soft lamp

**Wind-down "no" list:**
- Bright overhead lights
- Tablets / phones / TV
- New questions about tomorrow
- Sugar
- Surprise tasks

**Permission line:** *ADHD brains take longer to drop into sleep. Start the wind-down 60 minutes before lights off.*

**LAYOUT:** Vertical 5-step timeline at the top. Below: side-by-side YES (sage tint) / NO (terracotta tint) mini-cards. Permission line in butter-yellow strip at the bottom.

**The "one rule" of supporting an ADHD goal:**
> Reward starting, not finishing. The hardest part of an ADHD goal is the first 5 minutes. If they show up to the goal, the goal is winning.

**Permission slip:**
> I am allowed to lower the size of this goal. I can lower the time. I can lower the standard. I can change what counts as a win. The point is the practice, not the perfection.
