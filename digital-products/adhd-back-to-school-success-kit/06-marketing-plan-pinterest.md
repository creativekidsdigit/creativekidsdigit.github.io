# 06 — Marketing Plan: Pinterest, Email, Blog, Instagram

**Doc purpose:** The complete cold-traffic + nurture plan to launch and sustain the ADHD Back-to-School Success Kit. Pinterest is the primary engine; everything else feeds or is fed by it.

---

## 1. Channel strategy (one paragraph)

**Pinterest** is the cold-traffic engine — 60% of net-new buyers will find this product through pins routed to blog posts. **Email** is the conversion engine — every Pinterest click should turn into an opt-in, then a buyer over 7–21 days. **Blog posts on the site** are the conversion hubs that hold the lead-magnet opt-in. **Instagram** is the trust/credibility layer — same content repurposed, no funnel pressure. **Etsy** is its own ecosystem with its own organic search; treat its discovery surface as bonus, not primary.

### 2.4 Pin-design rules

- **Size:** 1000 × 1500 px (2:3 ratio).
- **Color:** Stay in the brand palette; do not chase trendy palettes that don't match the product.
- **Typography:** 1 display font + 1 body font max. Headlines 6–8 visible words.
- **Hero zone:** Top third = headline. Middle third = visual peek of a kit page. Bottom third = brand mark + URL.
- **Faces:** Avoid stock-photo "perfect mom" faces. Use product mockups, kid hands, fridge/door scenes, or no faces at all.
- **Every pin gets** a unique URL (UTM-tagged) so we can attribute saves and clicks.

### 2.5 Pin description template (paste, swap brackets)

```

[Reframe sentence — explain the science gently.]
```

### 2.6 UTM tagging convention

`?utm_source=pinterest&utm_medium=pin&utm_campaign=bts2026&utm_content=[hook-#]-[variant-#]`

Example: `?utm_source=pinterest&utm_medium=pin&utm_campaign=bts2026&utm_content=hook01-v3`

This allows you to identify which hook + visual is converting and double down for the next season.

## 3. Email plan

### 3.1 Lead-magnet opt-in flow

**Opt-in placement:**
- Footer of every blog post
- Mid-post "give-and-take" (after the pain-reframe section)
- Sticky banner on the site
- Pinterest pin descriptions (for posts that contain the opt-in)

**Lead magnet:** Free 5-Page Preview (defined in `04-etsy-seo-package.md` §5).

### 3.2 Welcome sequence (7 emails over 14 days)

| Day | Subject line | Goal |
|---|---|---|
| 0 | Here's your free preview — and the one page to print first | Deliver value, build trust |
| 1 | Why September is so hard for ADHD kids (and 3 things that actually help) | Educate, deepen connection |
| 3 | One question: did the calendar make sense? | Engagement; replies = high-value |
| 5 | The mistake I made my first August (so you don't have to) | Story, vulnerability |
| 7 | If you want the full 26-page kit, here's $5 off through Sunday | First conversion ask |
| 10 | The Mega Bundle math — 5 toolkits for less than 2 | Anchor + bundle ascension |
| 14 | Last call on the launch price | Urgency close |

### 3.3 Post-purchase sequence (kit buyers)

| Day | Subject | Goal |
|---|---|---|
| 0 | Your kit is here — print THIS page tonight | Reduce regret, deliver value |
| 3 | How are the first 6-week steps landing? | Engagement |
| 7 | A free Confidence Card pack as a thank-you | Delight, share-trigger |
| 14 | Want the editable Canva file for the kit? | Add-on upsell |
| 21 | A 10% off coupon for the Mega Bundle | Ascension upsell |
| 30 | Will you tell us how it's going? (review request) | Etsy/Payhip review |

### 3.4 Cart-abandonment sequence (Shopify / Payhip)

| Time | Subject |
|---|---|
| 1 hour | Your ADHD Back-to-School Kit is still waiting |
| 24 hours | A free First-Day Confidence Card pack while you decide |
| 72 hours | Last chance: 10% off your kit (auto-applied) |

### 3.5 Email do's & don'ts

- **DO** write at 8th-grade reading level. Short paragraphs. One CTA per email.
- **DO** treat replies as gold — every reply is a research interview.
- **DON'T** send daily. Twice a week max during launch; once a week steady-state.
- **DON'T** discount publicly below the launch price. Coupons go to subscribers only.

## 4. Blog post plan (10 posts → 10 pin clusters)

Each post is 1,200–1,800 words, follows the same skeleton, and ends with the lead-magnet opt-in + a soft CTA to the kit.

### 4.1 Post skeleton

1. **Hook (one paragraph):** Name the pain in the parent's exact words.
2. **Reframe (two paragraphs):** Why this is biology, not behavior. Include one neuroscience reference.
3. **Three concrete shifts (the meat):** What to do this week. Bulleted, scannable.
4. **What it looks like in a real family:** A composite story (no real names).
5. **What to watch for:** Edge cases, when to ask for help.
6. **The free 5-page preview opt-in box.**
7. **The kit / bundle CTA at the bottom.**

### 4.2 The 10 posts

1. *Why your ADHD kid melts down at 3:42pm in September (and what to do about it)*
2. *The 6-week soft launch: how to ramp an ADHD brain into a new school year*
3. *The ADHD sleep reset: 14 days, 10-minute increments, no fights*
4. *The first-day plan we tape to the door (and 12 steps that get the kid out the door)*
5. *The letter every ADHD parent should send the teacher in week 1 (template inside)*
6. *504 vs. IEP — the quick reference every ADHD parent should have on the fridge*
7. *When your child won't go to school: the calm 5-step protocol*
8. *Green / yellow / red — how to read your ADHD kid at 3pm in the first month*
9. *The 4-step big-feelings plan for the first 4 weeks of school*
10. *You're allowed to lower the bar in September — a permission slip for ADHD parents*

> **Each post is a Pinterest hook anchor.** Same hook in the headline, same hook in the pin overlay. Symmetry across the funnel = higher click-throughs.

### 4.3 Internal-linking rule

Every blog post links to:
- 2 other posts in this list (related-reads block at the bottom)
- The free 5-page preview opt-in (mid-post + footer)
- The kit landing page (footer CTA)
- The bundle landing page (post-script line)

## 5. Instagram plan (low-pressure, high-trust)

Instagram is **not the conversion channel**. Use it to compound trust and to source content for Pinterest. Three weekly post types:

| Day | Format | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| Mon | Carousel: "5 things ADHD parents need to hear today" | Saves & shares |
| Wed | Reel: One mini-script from the kit (e.g., the 60-second mindset reset) | Reach |
| Fri | Story: Behind-the-scenes — building a page, mockup process | Connection |

**Bio CTA:** Link to the lead-magnet opt-in page (NOT the kit). Free → email → kit.

## 8. Risk + mitigation

| Risk | Mitigation |
|---|---|
| Pin saturation — Pinterest down-ranks repetitive content | 50 unique designs at launch; rotate hook variants weekly |
| Email list fatigue | Cap at 2 sends/week during launch, 1/week after |
| Negative review on Etsy early | Respond same-day, professionally, offer refund; pre-seed reviews from email list (with disclosure) |
| Algorithm change on Pinterest | Diversify: keep 30%+ of net-new buyers from email list and direct site |
| Burnout | Pre-schedule everything before Aug 1. Don't launch what you can't maintain on bad days. |

## 9. The "minimum viable" version (if time runs out before launch)

If you only have 7 days before launch, ship in this order — and only this:

1. Listing live on Etsy + 1 standalone landing page on the site.
2. Lead-magnet preview built and connected to email.
3. 5 Pinterest pins (1 per top hook) routing to ONE blog post.
4. Welcome email sequence days 0, 3, 7 (skip the rest).
5. One Instagram launch reel (record once, post once).

You can ship this in a week. The rest layers in over the 90-day roadmap (`07-90-day-commercialization-roadmap.md`).
