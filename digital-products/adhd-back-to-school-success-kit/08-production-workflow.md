# 08 — Reusable Product-Production Workflow

**Doc purpose:** A reusable, repeatable factory the solo creator can run for **every** future ADHD product (and non-ADHD products in this brand). The Back-to-School Success Kit was built using this workflow; future products clone it.

**Promise:** A new printable PDF product, listed and pinned on every channel, in **10 working days**.

---

## 1. The 7-stage factory

```
1. RESEARCH    →  2. OUTLINE    →  3. CONTENT    →  4. DESIGN
                                                       │
                                                       ▼
8. RETRO       ←  7. LAUNCH     ←  6. LISTING    ←  5. ASSETS
```

Each stage has a fixed deliverable, a fixed time budget, and a fixed checklist. Don't move forward until the stage's deliverable exists.

---

## 2. Stage 1 — Research (4 hours)

**Deliverable:** A 1-page "Product Brief" that answers:

- Who exactly is the buyer? (sharp persona)
- What pain are they paying us to solve?
- Where do they currently search? (Etsy / Pinterest / Google query examples)
- Who's already serving this and how?
- What's the gap — and what would make our product unmistakably different?
- What's the price band the market supports?

**Inputs to use:** Etsy top results, Pinterest top pins for 5 search queries, Amazon KDP top sellers, 20+ comments on relevant Instagram posts, 5 minutes of Google Trends.

**Done when:** You can write the product's promise sentence (the "elevator promise") in one breath.

**Template — the Product Brief (1 page):**
```
PRODUCT WORKING TITLE:
PROMISE (one sentence):
PRIMARY BUYER (1-line persona):
TOP PAIN:
3 KEYWORD QUERIES THEY USE:
COMPETITIVE GAP:
PRICE BAND ($low–$high):
LEAD-MAGNET HOOK:
GO/NO-GO DECISION:
```

---

## 3. Stage 2 — Outline (2 hours)

**Deliverable:** A page-by-page index. (See `01-product-outline.md` as the model.)

**Rules:**
- Premium kit: 20–28 pages. Standard toolkit: 10–15. Mini: 5–10.
- 6 sections max, regardless of total page count.
- Section 1 is always the parent guide / why-it-matters.
- Last 2 pages are always: parent self-care + a letter / closing.
- Every page has one sentence describing what the buyer does with it.

**Done when:** Someone unfamiliar with the project can read the outline and immediately know what to print first.

---

## 4. Stage 3 — Content (8 hours)

**Deliverable:** A `02-printable-content.md` doc — every word on every page, in voice, with `LAYOUT:` direction below each block.

**Voice rules (locked across all Fearless Creations products):**
- Calm, judgment-free, neuroscience-grounded.
- 8th-grade reading level for parent copy. 3rd-grade for kid copy.
- No "just" / "simply" / "all you have to do."
- No medical claims. No diagnoses. No clinical promises.
- Every page is usable in isolation.

**Content reuse library (build once, reference forever):**
- The 60-second parent mindset reset
- The Decompression Dial (G/Y/R)
- The "I'm worried" conversation map
- The 4-step big-feelings plan
- The Sunday reset 6-step
- Generic teacher email templates (8–12)

> Future products pull from this library. Don't rewrite proven content.

**Done when:** A 5-page random sample reads in voice and could ship as-is.

---

## 5. Stage 4 — Design (12 hours total, batched 4 hr × 3 days)

**Deliverable:** A finished print-ready PDF + a 5-page free preview lead magnet.

**Use:** `03-canva-design-prompts.md` as the template. Master file is reused across all products in the line.

**Day 1 (4 hr):** Set up the master, design pages 1–10.
**Day 2 (4 hr):** Design pages 11–20.
**Day 3 (4 hr):** Design pages 21–end. Production checklist. Export 3 PDFs (print, web, lead magnet).

**Reusable Canva master ingredients:**
- Color palette tokens
- 4 callout-box styles
- Page header bar template
- Letter / cover frame templates
- Cut-line pattern with scissors
- Standard worksheet line styles
- Checkbox row component
- Tracker grid component

**Done when:** Production checklist (§I in the design doc) is fully ticked.

---

## 6. Stage 5 — Assets (4 hours)

**Deliverable:** All visual marketing assets.

- 5 product mockups (per the design doc §J)
- 5 launch Pinterest pins (1 per top hook)
- 1 hero image for the listings (square + vertical crop)
- 1 Instagram launch reel (recorded once)
- 1 IG carousel (10 slides) — "What's inside the kit"
- 1 email-sequence header graphic

**Tools:** Canva for mockups + pins. Phone for IG reel.

**Done when:** Every channel has its hero image AND its first 5 Pinterest pins.

---

## 7. Stage 6 — Listings (3 hours)

**Deliverable:** Live listings on Etsy, Payhip, Shopify (or the brand site), AND a separate Mega Bundle SKU.

**Use:** `04-etsy-seo-package.md` verbatim. Don't write listings from scratch — fill in the brackets.

**Listing-launch checklist:**
- [ ] Etsy listing title, description, 13 tags, attributes, 10 images, video.
- [ ] Etsy bundle SKU live.
- [ ] Payhip listing live with same images + cross-sell.
- [ ] Shopify product page live with structured data.
- [ ] Lead-magnet opt-in page live + connected to email tool.
- [ ] Welcome sequence days 0/3/7 active.
- [ ] Coupon codes (EARLYBIRD, WELCOMEBACK, TEACHERLOVE) created.
- [ ] Refund / risk-reversal text added to every checkout page.

**Done when:** You can buy your own product end-to-end without friction.

---

## 8. Stage 7 — Launch (Day 1 + 14)

**Deliverable:** Public launch executed on the calendar from `07-90-day-commercialization-roadmap.md`.

**Daily rhythm during launch (90 min/day, 14 days):**
- 30 min Pinterest (3 new pins, repin top performer, engage)
- 30 min email + Etsy convo replies
- 30 min IG post or story

**Critical hits:**
- Day 1: Launch announcement email, IG reel, 3 launch pins.
- Day 3: Engagement email.
- Day 7: First conversion ask + price step-up at end-of-day.
- Day 10: Bundle email.
- Day 14: Last-call + lock standard pricing.

**Done when:** Sales targets met, pricing at standard, first reviews requested.

---

## 9. Stage 8 — Retro (1 hour, monthly)

**Deliverable:** A short monthly retro doc capturing:
- Which 3 hooks saved/clicked best on Pinterest.
- Which 3 emails had the highest engagement.
- Which 3 buyer phrases came back in replies and reviews — these become next month's pin overlays.
- One thing to stop doing.
- One thing to double down on.

**Done when:** The retro is filed and the next month's plan is one decision shorter than the previous month's.

## 10. Time + cost budget for one new product

| Stage | Time | Cash cost |
|---|---|---|
| 1. Research | 4 hr | $0 |
| 2. Outline | 2 hr | $0 |
| 3. Content | 8 hr | $0 |
| 4. Design | 12 hr | $0 (Canva Pro already paid) |
| 5. Assets | 4 hr | $0 |
| 6. Listings | 3 hr | ~$1.50 (Etsy listing fees × 2) |
| 7. Launch | 21 hr (90 min × 14 days) | ~$0–$50 (optional Pinterest ads) |
| 8. Retro | 1 hr/month | $0 |
| **Total to launch** | **54 hr** | **~$2** |

A solo creator can build one new product per quarter at this cadence, sustained with ~5 hours/week of maintenance on existing products.

## 11. Annual operating rhythm

The factory has a yearly cadence. Build the **next** product around it.

| Month | Focus | Why |
|---|---|---|
| Jan | Mid-Year Reset relaunch | Q2 reboot energy peaks |
| Feb | KDP variant launch (low season for digital) | Slower digital season; good for compiled book |
| Mar | New mini product (sensory / regulation) | Spring break mid-year stress |
| Apr | Refresh Pinterest pins for spring | New visual season |
| May | **Annual research refresh** (re-run §6 of `00-market-research.md`) | 8–10 weeks pre BTS |
| Jun | Build the next BTS product (factory cycle starts) | T-30 to launch |
| **Jul** | **BTS launch (this product)** | Peak season starts |
| **Aug** | **BTS launch (Phase 1 of roadmap)** | Highest sales month |
| Sep | Steady-state + IEP/504 pack | Phase 2 ramp |
| Oct | KDP paperback launch | Holiday shopping starts |
| Nov | Holiday tier pricing + Cyber 5 push | Highest CTR window |
| Dec | Light maintenance only | Rest. Plan next year. |

## 12. Failure modes & antidotes

| If this happens | Do this |
|---|---|
| Sales below target by Day 14 | Re-test alternative listing title (variant A or B). Don't drop price below $19 publicly — pour into pinning instead. |
| Pinterest impressions stall | New visual variants, not new content. The hook is fine; the look got tired. |
| Email open rates below 30% | Cut frequency in half. Send only when you have a story or a useful new asset. |
| Refund rate above 5% | Tighten the listing description so the kit's contents are unmistakable. Add 1 more screenshot. |
| Burning out at week 6 | Drop everything except the 5 highest-performing pins on auto-rotate. The funnel can carry one tired week. |
| Reviews requesting "missing" pages | Add the page in v1.1 release; email all past buyers the update for free. Goodwill > perfectionism. |

## 13. Templates to copy when starting product N+1

For your next ADHD-line product, copy this folder structure:

```
digital-products/
  [new-product-slug]/
    00-market-research.md          ← clone & re-validate top half; re-do §3 matrix
    01-product-outline.md          ← clone & rewrite the page index
    02-printable-content.md        ← rewrite from scratch (use voice rules + content library)
    03-canva-design-prompts.md     ← clone; only update page-specific prompts
    04-etsy-seo-package.md         ← clone; rewrite §1 keyword bank, §2.1 titles, §2.2 description
    05-bundle-strategy-and-pricing.md  ← clone; update product ladder
    06-marketing-plan-pinterest.md ← clone; rewrite §2.3 hook angles, §4.2 blog posts
    07-90-day-commercialization-roadmap.md  ← clone; date the calendar
    08-production-workflow.md      ← reference only; same workflow for every product
```

> **The factory is the product. The product is the system. Build one. Then re-skin.**
