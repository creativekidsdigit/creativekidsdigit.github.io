## PAGE 2 — Why Mornings Are Harder for ADHD Kids

**Headline:** It's Not Laziness. It's Executive Function.

**Opening:**
If every morning feels like dragging your child through mud, you're not doing anything wrong. ADHD brains struggle with mornings because of how they're wired — not because they don't care.

**Why mornings are uniquely hard:**
- **Sleep inertia** is stronger — ADHD brains take longer to "boot up"
- **Task sequencing** breaks down — "get dressed, brush teeth, eat breakfast" is 15+ micro-steps
- **Time blindness** — 5 minutes feels like 30 minutes
- **Transitions** are painful — every room change requires a new "start"
- **Decision fatigue** hits early — choosing clothes can freeze the brain

**Reframe callout:**
> Your child isn't choosing to be slow. Their brain is still loading.

**Checklist ("Morning Struggles — check what sounds familiar"):**
☐ Takes 20+ minutes to get out of bed
☐ Gets distracted mid-task (socks in hand, staring at wall)
☐ Meltdown over clothing choices
☐ Forgets steps they've done 100 times
☐ Running late every single day
☐ You end up yelling by 7:30am
☐ Leaves without essentials (lunch, homework, shoes)

## PAGE 3 — The 4-Zone Morning Routine

**Headline:** One Routine. Four Zones. Every Single Day.

**Intro:** ADHD brains need the *same sequence* every morning. Not flexibility — predictability. These four zones turn chaos into autopilot.

**Four zones as visual cards:**

☀️ **Zone 1: Wake-Up Zone** *(Bedroom — 10 min)*
- Gentle alarm (vibrating watch or sunrise clock)
- 2 minutes of stretching in bed
- Feet on floor → immediate small reward (music on, favorite drink)
- NO screens until Zone 4 is complete

🧺 **Zone 2: Body Zone** *(Bathroom + Bedroom — 15 min)*
- Toilet, wash face, brush teeth
- Clothes laid out the night before (no choosing!)
- Visual checklist on mirror
- Timer running (visible countdown)

🍳 **Zone 3: Fuel Zone** *(Kitchen — 15 min)*
- 2-3 breakfast options (pre-decided Sunday night)
- Eat at the same spot every day
- No distractions — TV off, phone away
- Water bottle filled

🎒 **Zone 4: Launch Zone** *(Front door — 10 min)*
- Backpack (packed night before) by the door
- Shoes on, jacket grabbed
- 3-item exit check: bag, lunch, homework
- Hug + one encouraging word → out the door

## PAGE 4 — Night-Before Prep Checklist

**Headline:** Win the Morning the Night Before.

**Intro:** 80% of morning chaos is preventable with 10 minutes of prep after dinner.

**Two-column checklist:**

*Column 1 — Kid Tasks (with parent)*
☐ Choose tomorrow's outfit (weather-check together)
☐ Pack backpack — homework, books, permission slips
☐ Fill water bottle and put in fridge
☐ Set out shoes by the door
☐ Review tomorrow's schedule (any specials? gym clothes?)

*Column 2 — Parent Tasks*
☐ Lay out breakfast ingredients or prep overnight oats
☐ Check school calendar (early dismissal? picture day?)
☐ Sign anything that needs signing
☐ Charge devices if needed
☐ Set YOUR alarm 15 minutes before theirs

**Callout:**
> The calmer your night-before is, the calmer your morning will be. This is not extra work — it's insurance.

## PAGE 6 — Transition Strategies

**Headline:** Getting Unstuck Between Steps

**Intro:** The hardest part of an ADHD morning isn't any single task — it's the *space between tasks*. Here's how to bridge those gaps.

**Six strategies:**

⏰ **Countdown Warnings**
"5 minutes until we leave the bedroom." Give 5, 3, and 1 minute warnings. Never spring a transition.

🎵 **Transition Songs**
One song = one task. "When this song ends, teeth should be brushed." Music creates a natural timer.

🏃 **Movement Bridges**
Between zones, do 5 jumping jacks or a silly walk. Movement resets the brain for the next task.

👁️ **Point-of-Performance Reminders**
Put the cue WHERE the action happens. Toothbrush note on the mirror. Shoe reminder on the door.

🧸 **Transition Object**
A stuffed animal or toy that "travels" with them zone to zone. "Bring Bear to breakfast."

🎯 **First-Then Board**
"First: get dressed. Then: 2 minutes of music." Immediate payoff visible before the task starts.

## PAGE 7 — Wake-Up Strategies That Actually Work

**Headline:** Gentle Ways to Start the Engine

**Intro:** Yelling "GET UP!" is the worst wake-up strategy for an ADHD brain. The stress hormones make everything slower, not faster.

**Strategies grid (2×4):**

🌅 **Sunrise alarm clock** — Light that gradually brightens over 20 min. Mimics natural wake-up.

🎶 **Favorite song as alarm** — Something they love, not jarring. Rotate weekly so it doesn't lose power.

⏱️ **Vibrating watch alarm** — Wrist buzz is less startling than noise. Many ADHD kids prefer it.

🧊 **Cold washcloth on face** — Quick sensory reset. Works in under 30 seconds.

💬 **Whispered countdown** — "I'll be back in 5 minutes. When I come back, feet on floor." No nagging — one calm statement.

☕ **Smell motivation** — Bacon, toast, hot chocolate. The nose wakes the brain before words do.

🎮 **Earned screen time** — "Get to Zone 4 by 7:30 and you earn 5 minutes of your tablet." Visible reward.

🤝 **Co-regulation** — Sit on the bed. Rub their back. Be calm and present for 2 minutes before expecting action.

## PAGE 8 — Morning Meltdown Prevention

**Headline:** Catch It Before 7:30am

**Early warning signs:**
- Moving slower and slower (shutdown mode)
- Tears over small decisions (overwhelm)
- "I don't want to go to school" (avoidance)
- Aggression toward siblings (dysregulation)
- Hiding under blankets (freeze response)

**The Reset Protocol:**
1. **Pause the clock** — "We have time. Let's slow down for 60 seconds."
2. **Lower demands** — Shrink the task. "Just put on pants. I'll handle the rest."
3. **Offer co-regulation** — "I'm going to sit here with you. Take 3 breaths with me."
4. **Restart from current zone** — Don't go back. Start where they are.
5. **Skip if needed** — Messy hair won't ruin their education. Battles will.

**What to say / What to avoid:**

| ❌ Avoid | ✅ Try Instead |
|---|---|
| "We're going to be LATE!" | "Let's do the next small thing." |
| "Why can't you just get ready?" | "Your brain is still warming up." |
| "Your sister is already done." | "You're doing your morning your way." |
| "I'm not telling you again!" | "What's the one next step?" |
| "Fine, forget it." | "We'll figure this out together." |

## PAGE 10 — Emergency Morning Plan + Encouragement

**Headline:** When the Morning Falls Apart — A Plan

**Opening:** Some mornings, nothing works. The routine breaks. Tears happen. You're late. Here's the plan for THOSE mornings.

**4-step emergency protocol:**

🛑 **Step 1 — Triage**
☐ What MUST happen? (Clothed. Fed. At school.) Focus only on that.
☐ Skip everything non-essential. Messy hair? Fine. Forgot water bottle? School has fountains.

🧘 **Step 2 — Regulate Yourself First**
☐ 3 deep breaths before speaking.
☐ Lower your voice, not raise it.
☐ Your calm is their anchor.

🚗 **Step 3 — Get Out the Door**
☐ Breakfast bar in the car.
☐ Shoes carried, put on at school.
☐ "We'll reset tomorrow."

💛 **Step 4 — Reconnect After School**
☐ "This morning was hard for both of us."
☐ "I love you no matter what the morning looks like."
☐ Do the night-before prep together tonight.
