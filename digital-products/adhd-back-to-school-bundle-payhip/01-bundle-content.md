# 01 — Bundle Content (All 11 Printables)

**Doc purpose:** Full text content + layout direction for every printable in the ADHD Back-to-School Bundle. The design file (Canva) pulls copy from here verbatim.

**Total pages:** 14 (most printables = 1 page; 3 are 2 pages).

**Format:** US Letter, 8.5 × 11 in, 300 DPI, single combined print-ready PDF, plus each printable also exportable as a standalone PDF for mini-product sales.

**Audience:** Parents of ADHD kids ages 6–16. Parent-usable; not teacher-facing.

---

## A. ADHD-friendly design system (locked across all 11 printables)

| Element | Spec |
|---|---|
| **Visual rule** | One concept per page. One column. No more than 6 visible chunks per page. |
| **Color palette** | Primary sage `#A8C5A8`, cream `#FAF6EE`, terracotta `#D49A7C`, butter `#E8D88A`, charcoal text `#3A3A3A`. Soft, low-stimulation. |
| **Typography** | Headings: Fraunces 600/700. Body: Inter 400/600. Kid-facing accent: Caveat 500. Max 2 type sizes per page. |
| **Whitespace** | 0.75-in margins. 1.6+ line height. No page is more than 60% covered in ink. |
| **Icons** | Simple line icons. One icon per row max. Same icon set throughout. |
| **Color load** | One accent color per page, not three. Tinted callouts (10–15% tint), not saturated blocks. |
| **Reusability** | Designed to laminate and reuse with dry-erase. Every fillable line uses a soft gray `#E0DCD2` rule. |
| **Reading level** | Adult instructions: 7th-grade. Kid-facing prompts: 3rd-grade, present tense, "I" statements. |
| **No-no list** | No clipart cartoons, no stock photos, no rainbow gradients, no clutter, no busy patterns, no decorative borders that compete with content. |

> **Rule:** Every page should pass the "5-second test" — a parent who is overwhelmed at 9pm should know what to do with the page in 5 seconds.

### Printable 1 — Morning Routine Chart (1 page)

**Header (kid-facing):** My Morning

**Subhead (parent-facing, small):** Same steps. Same order. Every day.

**Body — 8 visual steps in a 2×4 grid (icon + 2–4 word label):**

1. **Wake up** — open blinds, lamp on
2. **Bathroom** — pee, brush teeth
3. **Drink water** — one full glass
4. **Get dressed** — clothes ready from last night
5. **Eat breakfast** — protein + fruit
6. **Pack what's left** — lunch, water bottle
7. **Backpack on** — shoes, jacket
8. **Out the door** — hug, deep breath

**Footer fillable line:** Today's bus / leave time: __________

**Parent note (small italic at the bottom):** Laminate this and post it inside your child's bedroom door. The order is the magic, not the speed.

**LAYOUT:** 2×4 grid taking 80% of the page. Each tile: cream background, sage border, large numeral 1–8 in Fraunces 700, 32pt, simple icon, 2–4 word label in Caveat 18pt. Header at the top in Fraunces 700, 36pt. Footer bus-time line and parent note at the bottom.

### Printable 2 — Bedtime Routine Chart (1 page)

**Header (kid-facing):** My Bedtime

**Subhead (parent-facing):** Wind down in 5 steps. Lights low. No new demands.

**Body — 5 visual steps in a vertical stack:**

1. **Snack & water** — small protein snack, one glass of water
2. **Bath or shower** — warm, dim light, no screens
3. **Pajamas + brush teeth** — set out tomorrow's clothes
4. **Quiet input** — book, audiobook, drawing, lego (no screens)
5. **Lights low + connection** — story, song, or 5 minutes with my person

**Wind-down "yes" list:**
- Audiobook
- Weighted blanket
- Stuffed animal
- White noise / fan
- Soft lamp

**Wind-down "no" list:**
- Bright overhead lights
- Tablets / phones / TV
- New questions about tomorrow
- Sugar
- Surprise tasks

**Parent note:** ADHD brains take longer to drop into sleep. Start the wind-down 60 minutes before "lights off."

**LAYOUT:** Vertical timeline of 5 numbered steps with icons (snack, bath, toothbrush, book, moon). Below: two side-by-side mini-cards — sage tinted "YES" with checkmarks, terracotta tinted "NO" with crosses. Parent note in butter-yellow callout strip at the bottom.

### Printable 6 — School Supply Checklist (1 page)

**Header:** ADHD-Friendly School Supply Checklist

**Subhead:** Get the school's list. Then get THIS list — the things that actually work for an ADHD brain.

**Body — 5 categories:**

**WRITING TOOLS**
- ☐ Pencils my child can grip without cramping
- ☐ Erasable pens (if allowed)
- ☐ Highlighters in 3 colors (one per priority level)
- ☐ Mechanical pencil with extra lead
- ☐ Pencil sharpener that holds shavings

**ORGANIZATION**
- ☐ Folder for each subject — different colors
- ☐ Binder with reinforced rings
- ☐ Plastic sleeve for "papers to take home"
- ☐ Small accordion folder for "to be filed"
- ☐ Sticky notes (small + large)
- ☐ Index cards
- ☐ Page flags / tabs

**FOCUS TOOLS**
- ☐ Fidget that's allowed at school (check the rules first)
- ☐ Noise-reducing headphones
- ☐ Wobble cushion (if teacher allows)
- ☐ Visual timer for home use
- ☐ Pencil-top fidget topper

**SENSORY TOOLBOX**
- ☐ Refillable water bottle (no leaks)
- ☐ Lunch container kid can open without help
- ☐ A backup snack
- ☐ Spare T-shirt + underwear (for younger kids)
- ☐ Small lotion or chapstick (sensory comfort)

**HOME-OFFICE FOR HOMEWORK**
- ☐ Desk lamp with warm light
- ☐ Visual timer
- ☐ Wireless headphones for focus music
- ☐ Whiteboard + 3 markers
- ☐ Calendar for the wall
- ☐ "Do not disturb" sign

**Parent note:** Don't buy everything. Highlight what your child actually struggles with and start there.

**Body:**

This bundle is **11 printables in one PDF**. You don't need to print them all today. Print the one page that matches the hardest part of your week, and start there.

**Most families print in this order:**
1. **Tonight:** Cover + How-to-Use + the printable that fits your hardest moment.
2. **This weekend:** Morning routine, bedtime routine, weekly planner.
3. **Sunday before school:** Backpack checklist, supply checklist.
4. **First week of school:** Homework planner, focus tracker, reward chart.
5. **First month:** Emotion regulation, parent–teacher log, goal-setting.

Every page is designed to **laminate and reuse with dry-erase**. Print once. Use all year.

**One rule:** If a page isn't working after 7 days, stop using it. Try a different page. The bundle is a menu, not a homework assignment.

## D. KDP-friendliness check (each printable)

For Phase 4 KDP adaptation, verify each printable still works in **black-and-white interior** (KDP standard). All 11 printables in this bundle were intentionally designed with:

- ✓ No color-dependent meaning (every accent is also a shape, label, or position)
- ✓ Single-column layouts (KDP-friendly)
- ✓ No bleed elements outside 0.5-in margin (KDP requires inner gutter)
- ✓ Plain fillable lines that print legibly in B&W
- ✓ Icons that are line-art (not filled blocks) so they don't muddy in B&W
