# 05 — Business Strategy

**Doc purpose:** The business architecture behind the bundle — pricing, upsells, follow-up products, ecosystem scaling, and the repeatable factory loop that turns this single launch into a steady-state product line.

---

## 1. Best Payhip pricing strategy (single vs bundle)

### 1.1 The principle

Payhip rewards **clear, ascending price tiers** — buyers see the value gap and naturally walk up the ladder. Avoid cluttered pricing (5+ SKUs at similar prices) and avoid heavy discounting (it trains buyers to wait).

### 1.2 The tier architecture

| Tier | SKU | Format | Price (launch / standard / holiday) |
|---|---|---|---|
| 0 | Free 3-page preview | 3-page PDF | $0 (lead magnet only) |
| 1 | Single mini printable | 1–2 page PDF | $4 / $5 / $5 |
| 2 | 3-pack mini bundle | 3–5 page PDF | $9 / $11 / $11 |
| 3 | **Full Back-to-School Bundle** | **14-page PDF** | **$17 / $24 / $29** |
| 4 | Mega Bundle (this + 4 toolkits) | 80+ page combined | $39 / $49 / $59 |
| 5 | Editable Canva master | Add-on file | +$9 |
| 6 | Multi-family / classroom license | License | +$19 |

### 1.3 Single vs bundle — which to lead with on Payhip

**Lead with the bundle.** Pin the Full Bundle (Tier 3) as the headline product on your Payhip store. Reasons:

1. **Higher AOV.** $17 launch vs $4 single — same traffic, 4× the gross.
2. **Lower refund rate.** Bundle buyers feel they got more than they paid for; single-item buyers feel they got "just a sheet."
3. **Clearer search positioning.** "ADHD Back-to-School Bundle" outranks "Morning Routine Chart" in Pinterest search.
4. **Lighter ops.** One product page to maintain, one delivery flow, one review thread.

Keep the singles live as **conversion bridges**, not as primary offers. Their job is to:
- Catch indecisive buyers ($4 is easier to say yes to than $17).
- Feed the upsell ladder (every single buyer gets a same-day "upgrade to the bundle for $13" offer).
- Hold separate Pinterest pins that target hyper-specific keywords ("adhd morning routine chart" is a higher-volume search than "adhd back to school bundle").

### 1.4 The "buyer's three doors" framing

Use this framing in every email and Pinterest pin description:

> **The "I just need ONE thing tonight" door** → $4 single printable.
>
> **The "I want a real plan for the school year" door** → $17 Back-to-School Bundle.
>
> **The "I want the whole ADHD school year handled" door** → $39 Mega Bundle.

Three doors, ascending value. Buyers self-select.

### 1.5 Pricing rules

- **Floor:** never publicly discount the bundle below $17. Email-only coupons can dip to $12.
- **Ceiling:** raise prices once a year, on July 1, before BTS season. Don't drop prices off-season.
- **Bundle anchor:** always show the sum-of-parts price next to the bundle price (`$73 → $39, save $34`). Render this as a graphic on the listing.
- **No bundles cheaper than the kit + 1 single:** if your Mega Bundle costs less than the Back-to-School Bundle + a single, you've broken your own anchor.
- **Coupon hygiene:** every coupon code is single-use per email and time-limited (max 7 days). Public codes cap at 10% off.

## 2. Upsell strategy (mini products → main bundle)

### 2.1 The funnel architecture

```
COLD TRAFFIC (Pinterest)
    │
    ▼
[Blog post — solves one problem, free]
    │
    ▼
[Free 3-page preview]  ◄── lead magnet, captures email
    │
    ▼ (immediately on confirmation page)
[$4 single printable]  ◄── tripwire: "Grab the chart you came for"
    │
    ▼ (Day 0 — immediate post-purchase upsell)
[$17 Back-to-School Bundle]  ◄── "Get all 11 for the price of 4"
    │
    ▼ (Day 7 — email)
[$24 Standard Bundle price + 10% returning customer code]
    │
    ▼ (Day 14 — email)
[$39 Mega Bundle]  ◄── "Add 4 ADHD toolkits"
    │
    ▼ (Day 30 — email)
[Editable Canva master / Multi-family license]  ◄── add-ons
```

### 2.2 The four key upsell moments

**Moment 1 — Lead-magnet confirmation page:** "While you wait for your preview email — grab the printable you actually came for, $4."

**Moment 2 — Single-item post-purchase upsell (one-click):** "Add the full bundle for $13 more (instead of $17 from scratch)." This converts ~25% of single buyers to the bundle.

**Moment 3 — Bundle confirmation page:** "Upgrade to the Mega Bundle for $22 more." Converts ~10–15% of bundle buyers.

**Moment 4 — Day 30 email:** "If the bundle worked, here's the next ADHD toolkit you'll want." Converts ~5–8% to the next standalone product.

### 2.3 Order-bump checklist (offered at checkout, not after)

- ✓ **Editable Canva master** (+$9) — for parents who want to customize fonts, colors, language. Take rate: 12–18%.
- ✓ **Multi-family license** (+$19) — for therapists, OTs, classroom teachers. Take rate: 3–6% (small but high-margin).
- ✓ **Free Confidence-Card pack** (+$0) — order-form value-add, not a true upsell. Increases perceived generosity and review rate.

### 2.4 Email cadence to drive upsells (14-day version)

| Day | Subject | Goal |
|---|---|---|
| 0 | Your bundle is here — print THIS page tonight | Reduce regret, show value |
| 3 | Did the morning chart help? (One question) | Engagement; replies = research interviews |
| 7 | Free Confidence-Card pack as a thank-you | Delight, share-trigger |
| 14 | Want the editable Canva file? Here's $3 off it. | Add-on upsell |
| 21 | Upgrade to the Mega Bundle for $22 | Ascension |
| 30 | Will you tell us how it's going? (Review request) | Reviews |

### 2.5 The "no-brainer" math (use in every upsell email)

> Single chart on its own: $4
> All 11 charts in the bundle: $17
> The math: $17 / 11 = **$1.55 per printable.**
> The math gets ridiculous fast.

Showing per-unit math turns the bundle into the obvious choice. Use it in the bundle's listing description, in the upsell email, and on the confirmation page.

## 3. Three follow-up product ideas

After the BTS Bundle launches, line up the next 3 products in this order. Each one reuses content frameworks, can be built in 8–14 hours each, and feeds the same email + Pinterest funnel.

### 3.1 Follow-up #1 — ADHD IEP / 504 Parent Advocacy Pack

**Tagline:** *Every form, letter, and script an ADHD parent needs to advocate at school.*

**What's inside (10–12 pages):**
- IEP vs 504 quick-reference card
- Written request for evaluation (letter template)
- Parent meeting prep worksheet
- 10 ready-to-send teacher emails (template bank)
- Accommodations menu (printable, hand to school)
- Goal-tracking sheet for IEP/504 goals
- Meeting recap template
- Annual review prep sheet

**Pricing:** $14 launch / $19 standard. Mini bundle add-on at $9.

**Why this is the right next product:** It's the highest-search-volume ADHD parenting pain point that the Back-to-School Bundle deliberately *doesn't* deeply solve. Parents who buy the bundle in August often need this in October when the first parent-teacher conference happens. Ships natively into the Day 30 upsell email.

**Build time:** 10 hours (most of the content is already in the existing brand voice; new design only).

### 3.2 Follow-up #2 — ADHD Sensory Toolkit & Calm-Down Kit Builder

**Tagline:** *Build the perfect sensory kit for your ADHD child — at home, at school, and in the car.*

**What's inside (8–10 pages):**
- Sensory profile builder (kid-facing)
- "Calm-down kit" builder for 3 locations (bedroom, backpack, car)
- Sensory-friendly classroom request letter (parent → teacher)
- Sensory snack list
- Sound, light, smell, touch, taste audit per location
- 7-day sensory tracker
- Sensory-overload emergency plan
- Permission slip: "It's okay if my kid needs THIS."

**Pricing:** $11 launch / $14 standard.

**Why this is the right next product:** Sensory is the second-highest ADHD parenting pain point after focus, and unlike "advocacy" it's a year-round need (not seasonal). It's also a strong cross-sell into a future "ADHD Holiday Sensory Survival" product in November–December.

**Build time:** 8 hours.

### 3.3 Follow-up #3 — ADHD Mid-Year Reset (Q2 Reboot Bundle)

**Tagline:** *January reset. Same family. Same kid. Different start.*

**What's inside (12–14 pages):**
- Mid-year reflection (kid + parent)
- "What's working / what's not" audit
- New routines for the back-half of the year
- A 30-day reset calendar
- Updated reward chart
- Updated focus tracker
- Updated weekly planner with mid-year goals
- A letter to the parent for January

**Pricing:** $14 launch / $19 standard. Bundles into the Mega Bundle without raising the bundle price.

**Why this is the right next product:** January is the second-highest digital-printable buying season after August. Same audience. Same funnel. Different pain point (mid-year exhaustion vs. start-of-year prep). Lets the brand sell to the *same buyer twice* in a year.

**Build time:** 10 hours.

### 3.4 Follow-up product sequencing on the calendar

| Launch month | Product | Why this month |
|---|---|---|
| Aug (this launch) | ADHD Back-to-School Bundle | Peak BTS season |
| Sep | ADHD IEP/504 Advocacy Pack | First parent-teacher conferences happen mid-Oct |
| Oct–Nov | ADHD Sensory Toolkit | Holiday sensory overwhelm hits in Nov–Dec |
| Jan | ADHD Mid-Year Reset Bundle | Q2 reboot energy peaks |

That's 4 products in 5 months, with one solo creator, on a single Payhip store.

### 4.4 What to expand into (after the line is healthy)

In priority order:

1. **Audio product** — a 60-min companion podcast / audiobook of the Parent Letter + scripts. Sold on Payhip + Spotify-for-podcasters. Low maintenance, high authority.
2. **Workshops / mini-courses** — a 90-min recorded workshop "How to use the ADHD Back-to-School Bundle" for $39. Reuses existing content; different format.
3. **Printed-and-shipped subscription** (Phase 5) — quarterly mailed bundle of seasonal printables. High margin, harder ops. Only if email list > 7k.
4. **Affiliate income** — recommend a small set of ADHD-friendly products (timers, fidgets, weighted blankets) with affiliate links inside the bundle and emails. Passive.

Don't do all four in year 1. Pick one for year 2.

## 5. The repeatable product factory

### 5.1 The 6-stage factory loop (every product runs through this)

```
1. SCOUT       →  2. OUTLINE   →  3. CONTENT
                                       │
                                       ▼
6. RETRO       ←  5. LAUNCH    ←  4. ASSETS+LIST
```

Each stage has a fixed deliverable, a fixed time budget, and a fixed checklist. Don't move forward until the stage's deliverable exists.

### 5.2 Time + cost budget per new product

| Stage | Time | Tools |
|---|---|---|
| 1. Scout (research + 1-page brief) | 3 hr | Pinterest search, Payhip browse, Google Trends |
| 2. Outline (page-by-page index) | 1 hr | Reuse template |
| 3. Content (full text doc, in voice) | 6 hr | Markdown |
| 4. Assets + Listing (design, mockups, listing pages) | 8 hr | Canva master |
| 5. Launch (14-day Pinterest + email) | 8 hr (active) | Pre-scheduled |
| 6. Retro (1-page learnings doc) | 1 hr | Markdown |
| **Total** | **27 hr** | |

A solo creator running this factory at this cadence ships **a new product every 4–6 weeks** at steady state.

### 5.3 Reusable assets (the "factory inventory")

The first product builds these assets. Every product after just clones them.

- Canva master file (palette + components)
- Email welcome sequence (swap in product-specific subject lines)
- Pinterest pin templates (10 visual variants, swap headlines)
- Blog post template (hook → reframe → 3 shifts → opt-in → CTA)
- Payhip listing template (swap §1.3 description)
- Shopify listing template (swap HTML body)
- KDP interior template (swap content)
- Mockup set (swap product file in)

> **Rule:** if a deliverable exists more than once across products, it lives in the factory inventory and is *referenced*, not rewritten.

### 5.4 The "clone this folder" template for product N+1

When starting your next product, copy this folder structure:

```
digital-products/
  [new-product-slug]/
    01-bundle-content.md             ← rewrite for new product (use voice rules)
    02-design-system-and-mockups.md  ← clone; only update §B page-prompts
    03-payhip-listing-and-platforms.md ← clone; rewrite §1.3 description
    04-pinterest-14-day-launch.md    ← clone; rewrite §4 hooks (28 new)
    05-business-strategy.md          ← clone; only update §3 follow-ups
    README.md                        ← clone; update phase-to-file map
```

Six files. Three of them are mostly clone-and-replace. Three need real rewriting.

### 5.6 Failure modes & antidotes

| If this happens | Do this |
|---|---|
| Sales below target by Day 14 | Re-test Payhip alt title (§1.1). Don't drop price below $17 publicly — pour into pinning instead. |
| Pinterest impressions stall | New visual variants, not new content. The hook is fine; the look got tired. |
| Email open rates drop below 30% | Cut frequency in half. Send only when you have a story or a useful new asset. |
| Refund rate above 5% | Tighten the listing description so the bundle's contents are unmistakable. Add 1 more screenshot. |
| Burning out at week 4 | Drop everything except the 5 highest-performing pins on auto-rotate. The funnel can carry one tired month. |
| Competitor copies your bundle | Don't fight on Etsy — you don't sell there. Compete on voice, design, and the email list they can't copy. |
| Reviews requesting "missing" pages | Ship a v1.1 update; email past buyers free. Goodwill > perfectionism. |

### 5.7 The factory's success metric

After 12 months, a healthy factory looks like:

- 4–6 products live across the line
- 5,000+ email subscribers
- 100+ Pinterest pins, top 10 driving 80% of saves
- 50+ Payhip reviews
- 20+ hours/month freed up vs. month 1
- One product launching every 4–6 weeks at predictable conversion rates

> **The factory is the product. The bundle is a sample of what the factory makes.**
