## Target Competency / Final Objective

By the end of the lesson, learners will be able to **interpret a historical text about an ancient civilization, identify its key features (location, achievements, decline) and produce a short written reaction justifying their personal opinion.**

> *Can-do statement:* "I can read a historical text, locate specific information, infer meaning from context, and write a short response that supports my opinion with evidence from the text."

---

## Learning Objectives (SMART + Bloom's Taxonomy)

By the end of the lesson, learners will be able to:

1. **(Remember)** Identify at least 5 factual details about the Aztec civilization from the text.
2. **(Understand)** Interpret 4 contextual vocabulary items related to civilization and history.
3. **(Apply)** Use scanning &amp; skimming strategies to answer comprehension questions in &lt; 10 minutes.
4. **(Analyze)** Distinguish facts from inferences in a historical text.
5. **(Evaluate / Create)** Produce a 4&ndash;5-sentence written reaction justifying a personal opinion with textual evidence.

---

## Cross-curricular &amp; Transversal Competencies

- **Intellectual:** critical thinking, inference, comparison
- **Methodological:** reading strategies (skimming, scanning, intensive reading), note-taking
- **Communicative:** expressing opinions in writing, justifying ideas with evidence
- **Personal &amp; social:** respect for cultural heritage, awareness of historical diversity

## Lesson Stages

| Stage | Time | Teacher's Activity | Pupils' Activity | Interaction | Aim |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Warm-up** | 5' | Greets pupils. Shows the eliciting question on the board: *"Which ancient civilizations have you heard about?"* Writes 3&ndash;4 names elicited on the board. | Brainstorm orally; recall names: *Egyptians, Greeks, Romans, Carthaginians, Aztecs&hellip;* | T &harr; Ps | Activate schema; engage learners; introduce the topic. |
| **Lead-in** | 5' | Shows / sketches a map of the Americas. Asks: *"Where would you place the Aztec civilization?"* Pre-teaches 4 key items: **civilization, empire, ruins, decline** (using definition + visuals + example sentences). Concept-checks: *"Is a civilization a small village? &rarr; No. Are ruins old or new? &rarr; Old."* | Locate Mexico on the map; repeat new lexis chorally then individually; answer CCQs. | T &harr; Ps | Pre-teach essential lexis; lower the affective filter before reading. |
| **While-reading 1** *(skim for gist)* | 8' | Distributes the text. Sets the task: *"Read quickly &mdash; you have 3 minutes. Answer: What is the text mainly about?"* Sets ICQs: *"Are we reading every word? Do we have a lot of time?"* | Skim the text individually; choose between three options on the board (a/b/c); compare with a partner. | Individual &rarr; P &harr; P | Develop skimming sub-skill; check global understanding. |
| **While-reading 2** *(scan for specific info)* | 12' | Sets 5 comprehension questions: 1) Where did the Aztecs live? 2) When was their empire at its peak? 3) Name 2 achievements. 4) Who conquered them? 5) Find a synonym for "fall". | Scan the text, write short answers in pairs, justify with line references. | P &harr; P | Develop scanning sub-skill; train evidence-based reading. |
| **Practice** *(intensive / inference)* | 10' | Sets 2 inference tasks: T/F + justify ("The Aztecs had a writing system" / "The conquest was peaceful"); 1 inference: *"Why does the writer use the word 'tragic' in line 18?"* Monitors and supports weaker pairs. | Reread the relevant paragraphs; discuss in pairs; justify orally. | P &harr; P | Move from lower-order to higher-order thinking (Bloom: Analyze). |
| **Production** *(written reaction)* | 15' | Sets the task: *"In 4&ndash;5 sentences, write your reaction: What lesson can today's world learn from the fall of the Aztec empire?"* Provides sentence starters on the board (*In my opinion&hellip; / The text shows that&hellip; / I think this is important because&hellip;*). Circulates and supports. | Plan ideas individually (2'); draft (10'); peer-edit using a 3-point checklist: opinion stated / evidence given / 1 connector used (3'). | Individual + P &harr; P | Develop written production; integrate text content with personal opinion (Bloom: Evaluate / Create). |
| **Wrap-up** | 5' | Invites 2&ndash;3 volunteers to read aloud. Gives quick oral feedback. Recaps key vocabulary. Sets homework. | Volunteers share; class listens; copy homework. | T &harr; Ps | Closure; reinforce learning; check affective state. |

**Total: 60 minutes.**

## Expected Pupils' Answers

**Skim for gist:** *(b) The Aztec civilization and its decline.*

**Scan questions:**
1. In central Mexico (around Tenochtitl&aacute;n).
2. The 14th&ndash;16th century.
3. Floating gardens, accurate calendar, advanced agriculture, monumental architecture *(any 2)*.
4. The Spanish conquistadors led by Hern&aacute;n Cort&eacute;s.
5. *Decline* / *collapse*.

**Inference:**
- T/F: "The Aztecs had a writing system." &rarr; **True** *("They used pictographic writing&hellip;" line 12).*
- "The conquest was peaceful." &rarr; **False** *("violent invasion" / "tragic end" lines 18&ndash;19).*
- Inference on "tragic": the writer expresses regret over the loss of culture, not just the military defeat.

**Written reaction (sample):**
> *In my opinion, the fall of the Aztec empire teaches us that no civilization is permanent. The text shows that even powerful empires can collapse when they meet a stronger force. I think this is important because today's nations should value cultural diversity and protect heritage sites for future generations.*

## Differentiation Strategies

| Tier | Strategy |
|---|---|
| **Struggling learners** | Provide a glossary box on the handout; pair with a stronger peer; allow 3 of 5 scan questions; sentence starters mandatory in production. |
| **On-level learners** | Standard task; encourage at least 1 connector and 1 piece of textual evidence in production. |
| **Advanced learners** | Optional starred questions: *"Compare the Aztec civilization with one other ancient civilization you know"*; encourage 2 connectors and direct quotation in production. |

## Assessment

**Formative (during the lesson):**
- Monitoring pair work during scan and inference stages.
- Quick oral CCQs after the lead-in.
- Peer-editing checklist (opinion / evidence / connector).

**Summative (end-of-lesson task &mdash; written reaction):**

| Criterion | 0 | 1 | 2 |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Task fulfillment** | Off-topic / no opinion | Opinion stated, weak evidence | Clear opinion + textual evidence |
| **Organization** | No structure | Some logical order | Clear introduction &rarr; reasoning &rarr; conclusion |
| **Grammar &amp; vocabulary** | Frequent errors block meaning | Errors but meaning clear | Few errors; uses lesson lexis |
| **Mechanics** | Many issues | Some issues | Spelling &amp; punctuation accurate |

**Total: /8** &mdash; converted to /20 for reporting.

## Remedial Activities

If &gt; 30% of pupils fail the production task:
1. Re-teach the opinion-justification structure with a guided model on the board.
2. Provide a writing frame:  *In my opinion, &hellip; because &hellip; For example, the text says &hellip;*
3. Re-assess with a shorter prompt (2&ndash;3 sentences) the following session.
4. Use bilingual support (Arabic / French) only for clarification of complex inference questions.

---

## Classroom Management Tips

1. **Time discipline.** Use a visible timer for each stage; announce 1-minute warnings before transitions.
2. **Pair formation.** Pre-arrange mixed-ability pairs (one strong + one developing reader) before the lesson to save time.
3. **Maximize STT (Student Talking Time).** During pair-check phases, sit at one corner of the room and let pupils self-correct &mdash; intervene only after 30 seconds.
4. **Manage the production stage.** Walk a fixed loop around the room; do not get stuck with one pair. Note 2&ndash;3 common errors for delayed correction at the wrap-up.
5. **Closing energy.** End with a positive recap: *"Today you read a real historical text, found specific information, and wrote your own reaction in English &mdash; well done."*

### *The Lost Civilization of the Aztecs*

<ol class="reading-lines">
<li>In the heart of central Mexico, around the great city of Tenochtitl&aacute;n,</li>
<li>there once flourished one of the most fascinating civilizations of the</li>
<li>Americas &mdash; the Aztec empire. Founded in the early 14th century, the Aztecs</li>
<li>built their capital on a small island in Lake Texcoco, and within two</li>
<li>hundred years they transformed it into a vast metropolis of palaces and markets.</li>
<li>At the peak of their power, between the 14th and the 16th century, the Aztecs</li>
<li>ruled over millions of people. Their society was highly organized, with priests,</li>
<li>warriors, farmers and skilled artisans. They were remarkable engineers and</li>
<li>scientists who left a deep mark on the region. They invented floating gardens</li>
<li>called <em>chinampas</em> to grow maize and vegetables directly on the surface of</li>
<li>the lake; they followed an accurate solar calendar and observed the stars;</li>
<li>they used a pictographic writing system to record laws, history and religion;</li>
<li>and they raised monumental architecture &mdash; pyramids, plazas and stone</li>
<li>sculptures &mdash; that still amaze visitors who walk among the ruins today.</li>
<li>Yet this brilliant civilization did not last. In 1519, Spanish conquistadors</li>
<li>led by Hern&aacute;n Cort&eacute;s arrived on the eastern coast and marched towards</li>
<li>Tenochtitl&aacute;n. After two years of fierce resistance, weakened by new diseases</li>
<li>brought from Europe, the city fell to a violent invasion that brought a tragic</li>
<li>end to centuries of Aztec rule. Temples were destroyed, ancient books were</li>
<li>burned, and a unique culture experienced a rapid decline.</li>
<li>The collapse of the Aztec empire reminds us that no civilization, however</li>
<li>powerful, is eternal. Just as Algerians honour the memory of Numidia and</li>
<li>Carthage in their own land, the modern world owes it to itself to study,</li>
<li>respect and protect what remains of these ancient peoples everywhere &mdash; for</li>
<li>each one is part of the shared heritage of humanity.</li>

### Tasks

**1. Skim for gist (3 min).** What is the text mainly about?
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;(a) The geography of Mexico.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;(b) The Aztec civilization and its decline.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;(c) Spanish exploration in the 16th century.

**2. Scan for specific information (12 min).**

1. Where did the Aztecs live?
2. When was their empire at its peak?
3. Name **two** Aztec achievements.
4. Who conquered them?
5. Find a synonym for the word **fall** in the text.

**3. Intensive reading &amp; inference (10 min).**

- True or false? Justify with a line reference.
&nbsp;&nbsp;a. *"The Aztecs had a writing system."*
&nbsp;&nbsp;b. *"The conquest was peaceful."*
- Why does the writer use the word **tragic** in line 18?

**4. Written reaction (15 min).** In 4&ndash;5 sentences, answer:
*What lesson can today's world learn from the fall of the Aztec empire?*

> *Sentence starters:* In my opinion&hellip; / The text shows that&hellip; / I think this is important because&hellip;

**Mini-glossary** *(for support)*: *civilization* = a developed society; *empire* = a large state ruled by one government; *ruins* = what remains of old buildings; *decline* = a slow fall; *conquistador* = a Spanish soldier-explorer of the 16th century.
