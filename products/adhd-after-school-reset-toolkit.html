The core method

The 60-Minute After-School Reset

Four zones. Same order. Every day. This is the system that changes everything.

🧗 Decompress 20 min — quiet, snack, no demands
→
🍎 Fuel 15 min — eat, hydrate, sit together
→
🏃 Move 15 min — physical play, outside if possible
→
🤝 Reconnect 10 min — connection cue, transition to homework

After this 60-minute protocol, your child’s brain has recovered enough self-regulation capacity to handle homework, chores, or family time. Skip it, and you’ll fight the rest of the evening.

Frequently asked questions

What age range is this for?

The toolkit is designed for parents of children ages 5–12 with ADHD. The strategies scale across this range — you’ll find age-specific guidance (e.g., the Sprint Method homework table has different timings for 5–6, 7–9, and 10–12 year-olds).

Does my child need a formal ADHD diagnosis?

No. If your child struggles with after-school meltdowns, emotional dysregulation, or homework resistance, this toolkit will help — diagnosis or not. Many families use it while awaiting assessment.

What is “after-school restraint collapse”?

It’s the phenomenon where children (especially neurodivergent kids) hold themselves together all day at school, then release all that suppressed stress the moment they get home. It looks like meltdowns, aggression, shutdown, or extreme irritability between 3–6pm.

How is this different from calm-down worksheets?

This isn’t just worksheets — it’s a complete system. It covers the entire after-school window: arrival, decompression, movement, reconnection, homework, rewards, AND emergency meltdown recovery. Plus parent co-regulation guides, sibling triage, and screen-time boundaries.

What format is the download?

High-resolution PDF, 300 DPI, US Letter (8.5 × 11 in). Print at home on any printer, or send to a local print shop. Many parents laminate the routine charts and calm-down cards for daily reuse.

Can I print it multiple times?

Yes! Print unlimited copies for your own family. The checklists, trackers, and check-in sheets are designed to be reprinted weekly.

Can teachers or therapists use this?

For personal classroom or single-client use, yes. If you need a multi-use license for a practice or school, contact us for our therapist/counselor license add-on.

What if the meltdowns don’t stop immediately?

Behavior change takes time. Most parents report noticeable improvement within 5–7 days of consistent use. The toolkit includes an Emergency Meltdown Plan for the days things still fall apart — and a Parent Reset page for when YOU need support too.
